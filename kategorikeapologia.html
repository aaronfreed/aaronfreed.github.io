Basic Information

Given this album’s origins, it’s necessarily something of a hodgepodge. Its tracks fit into three broad categories.

Πρώτη κᾰτηγορῐ́ᾱ

Prṓtē kătēgorĭ́ā

First Category

The first is “miscellaneous bonus tracks from its predecessors, Compositions 2023-2024 and Mūsica ex tempore malōrum”. I set a strict limitation of two audio CDs for both albums; as a result, four leftovers from around the same time didn’t fit into either album’s format for various reasons.⁽¹⁾ I’ve therefore collected them here for listeners’ convenience (as a great band once said, give me convenience or give me death). They are:

Τρῐ́τος κᾰτηγορῐ́ᾱ

Trĭ́tē kătēgorĭ́ā

Third Category

Finally, I’ve since written seven new songs (in varying stages of completion) that didn’t fit within the runtime:

  • “Nōtiō nivālis”
  • “The Pipeline, Filling in Time”
  • “Caput mortuum”
  • “Hae eunt ad ūndecim”
  • “Sexāgintā quattuor”
  • “Digitī”
  • “Celeritās Deī tibī̆, imperātor fusce”

These currently close out this album.

I do eventually plan to organize this album a bit better (the pitch-flipped songs may get an entirely separate album when there are enough of them), but until I finalize the mixes for Compositions 2023-2024 and Mūsica ex tempore malōrum, I don’t expect to have the mental energy to do so.

For the time being, I emphatically recommend that listeners new to my music start with something other than this collection (ideally, Selected Works, 2023-2024, Compositions 2023-2024 and Mūsica ex tempore malōrum). Nonetheless, I hope you enjoy it. (If you must start with this album, at least start with the new songs, though even many of those are mere sketches.)

The Etymology of the Apology

Album Title Translations

I’ve tentatively titled this album Κᾰτηγορῐκή ᾰ̓πολογῐ́ᾱ (romanized: Kătēgorĭkḗ ăpologĭ́ā), an ancient Greek phrase that can be translated in several ways, including Accusatory Apologia, Accusatory Defense, Predicational Apology, or Categorical Apology.

One reason I selected it is because in Greek, κᾰτηγορῐ́ᾱ (romanized: kategoria; modern form: κατηγορία, romanized as katigoría) and ᾰ̓πολογῐ́ᾱ (romanized: ăpologĭ́ā; modern form: απολογία, romanized as apología) are antonyms. Paradoxically juxtaposing them therefore felt especially pleasing.

I likewise find it pleasing that this title could effectively mean either defensive apology, defensive defense, prosecutorial defense, conditional apology, or unconditional apology. This leads to the question of “Which of these meanings is the intended sense?” I’m sure you’ve already predicted my answer, but if you need a hint, it’s a single three-letter word.⁽²⁾

And in case you’re wondering: yes, the English words “categoria”, “category” and “categorical” are respectively direct descendants of «κατηγορία» and «κᾰτηγορῐκός» (romanized: “kătēgorĭ́kós”; the root form of «κᾰτηγορῐκή»), although the latter two did not inherit their ancestors’ respective senses of “accusation” and “accusatory”; likewise, our words “apologia” and “apology” descend directly from «ᾰ̓πολογῐ́ᾱ».

In conclusion, language is a land of contrasts.

Credits

Composition

The following songs interpolate brief segments of other works:

  • “Below Cloud City”: Cream, “Badge” (Eric Clapton/George Harrison) – interpolates main bass riff once purely for the lols
  • “Sexāgintā quattuor”: Nightwish, “The Poet and the Pendulum” (Tuomas Holopainen) – the chord progression from 2:26-2:44 is adapted more or less wholesale from “White Lands of Empathica”
Apart from that, all songs are entirely my work.
