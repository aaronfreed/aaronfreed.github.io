Aaron Freed vs. the Risk of Rain Stems

I’ve made a couple of songs that incorporate some of Chris Christodoulou’s stems from RoR2. They aren’t quite complete yet, but they’re in strong enough states that I feel comfortable sharing them.

“Landing” / “The Rain Formerly Known as Purple” / “A Glacier Eventually Farts”

The first is a mash-up of “The Rain Formerly Known as Purple” and “A Glacier Eventually Farts” with Alexander Seropian’s “Landing” from Bungie’s Marathon (1994), which I made for a Marathon mod called Eternal. The moment I learned that Chris had released the RoR2 stems, I knew I’d inevitably make this mashup – I’d noticed the similarity between the melodies the very first time I heard “The Rain Formerly Known as Purple”. You’ll know what i mean the moment you get to the relevant point of this track. This is a very Blade Runner-inspired collaboration with another arranger who uses the name wowbobwow, and I’m very pleased with how different the RoR2 stems feel in this context.

(This also briefly interpolates the drum intro from Nirvana’s “Scentless Apprentice” at a few points, and the ending briefly quotes Mick Gordon’s “They Only Thing They Fear Is You”. Marathon Eternal, Doom Eternal, get it? I’m fairly sure I was legally obligated.)

I sent this to Chris earlier this week; his feedback was overall quite positive, and he provided some insightful suggestions that I’ll incorporate the next time I work on this mix. I’d been thinking of doing more mash-ups with Marathon songs, but he suggested instead writing original songs that incorporated his stems. I’d been too intimidated to even try that until he suggested it, but I sat down, loaded a few stems from “The Raindrop That Fell to the Sky” into Logic, and began writing parts – which I found surprisingly easy.

“Ὁ ζῐ́γγος αὔξεται μέγᾰς / Ὀνειροπολεῖς τῆς βῐαίᾱς αὐξήσεος”

The second track, which I’m writing for a different Marathon mod called Tempus Irae Redux, is the result. (On the very slim chance that anyone that reads this is familiar with the original Tempus Irae, I’m planning to use it on the level “Downward Spiral”.) It’s also not finished yet, but its basic skeleton has by now taken form. It’s divided cleanly into two movements, with a heavy mood shift between them; the dividing line is where I start also incorporating stems from “They Might as Well Be Dead”. this is a very jarring transition, and I think I like it that way (though I’ll probably add more reverb).

What shocked me is how perfectly the second half of “They Might as Well Be Dead” fit over the solo from the second half of “The Raindrop That Fell to the Sky” – I could tell there was probably enough harmonic overlap between them for a mash-up of the tracks to work, but I didn’t expect to just be able to loop parts from “They Might as Well Be Dead” over the entire solo once I’d pitch-shifted and time-stretched them. But that’s exactly what wound up happening. The result feels stylistically somewhere between Pink Floyd, Vangelis, Godspeed You! Black Emperor, and Nine Inch Nails (four of my favorite artists, incidentally).

Right now, the second half is entirely stems from the two RoR2 tracks, but I plan to write original parts to flesh out the arrangement further and add some more texture. I’ll also undoubtedly merge in elements of a few additional RoR tracks – definitely at least “Moisture Deficit”, and also “Once in a Lullaby” if I can get it to fit.

The titles are my undoubtedly amateurish attempts at translating “The whirring grows loud” and “You dream of violent growth” to ancient Greek, a language that I just barely know enough to be dangerous in. (Marathon is literally a Greek name, after all, so I find it especially fitting to use Greek for these titles.)

–Aaron Freed
–2024-08-10
