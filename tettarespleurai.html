Introduction

I write a lot of music: from June 2024 to April 2025 alone, I wrote or cowrote over five hours of original music. This collection contains four of my favorite tracks from 2024 to 2026. I’ve always loved old double LPs with one track to a side, so once I had that many side-length compositions, I felt obligated to collect them.

These tracks are, of course, atypical of my work in a few ways:

  • They’re about twelve minutes above my average track length. (It’s no coincidence that I think they’re all worth anthologizing: I only write songs that long if I think their quality justifies the length.)
  • «Κα­ται­βᾰ­τή ἕ­λῐξ» and “Om­nēs vi­ae Sō­lem dū­cunt” have vocal parts, which I sel­dom write.
  • While I’m known to insert brief quotes of other composers’ work as easter eggs, I rarely rewrite existing songs outright. But two of these tracks are heavily based on other composers’ work:
    • «Καταιβᾰτή ἕλῐξ» interpolates four of Chris Christodoulou’s tracks (Risk of Rain series, Deadbolt) with permission (for which I’m immensely grateful).
    • “Leela / Covenant Dance / 𓈖𓐍𓅱𓏏𓀁 𓈖𓏏 𓉡𓁥” is partly based on Alex Se­ro­pi­an’s “Leela” (Ma­ra­thon, 1994-12-21) and Martin O’Don­nell & Michael Salvatori’s “Covenant Dance” (Halo: Combat Evolved, 2001-11-15). I began writing it for Eternal and forgot to stop; in fact, I’m still not done.

But they also showcase many hallmarks of my style for most/all of their runtime:

  • Eschewal of verse/chorus structure
  • Unorthodox chord progressions
  • Complex, fill-laden drum parts
  • Compound time signatures
  • Complex arrangements employing dense arpeggiation
  • Long, gradual, dramatic dynamic shifts
  • Mostly instrumental, with lyrics only in foreign languages
  • Album/song/movement titles in dead languages or dialects (Latin, Attic Greek, Middle Egyptian)

My music as a whole is too stylistically diverse to fit cleanly into one genre, but this anthology can reasonably be described as progressive rock/metal and/or post-rock. My biggest conscious influences are:

  1. Johann Sebastian Bach
  2. Brian Eno
  3. The Beatles
  4. Pink Floyd
  5. Nobuo Uematsu
  6. Vangelis
  7. King Crimson
  8. Genesis (and related solo acts, e.g. Phil Collins and Peter Gabriel)

My other in­flu­en­ces include Steve Al­bi­ni, Beas­tie Boys, Black Sa­bbath, Chris Chris­to­dou­lou, John Col­trane, Mi­les Da­vis, Death­spell Ome­ga, Bob Dylan, Tim & Geoff Fol­lin, Mar­vin Gaye, Ge­ne­sis, Phi­lip Glass, God­speed You! Black Em­pe­ror, Joy Di­vi­sion, Kō­en­ji Hy­ak­kei, Ma­gma, Me­tal­li­ca, Ya­su­no­ri Mit­su­da, Jan­elle Mo­náe, Jo­an­na New­som, Night­wish, Nine Inch Nails, Steve Reich, Ren, Rush, Rin­go Shī­na, Slint, Talk Talk, Talk­ing Heads, Tears for Fears, Tool, the Vel­vet Un­der­ground, Win­dir, Yes, Neil Young, & Frank Zap­pa. Some of these are un­doub­ted­ly more ob­vi­ous in­flu­en­ces than oth­ers: e.g., the Beas­tie Boys’ in­flu­ence on my mu­sic is like­ly quite ob­lique, but Paul’s Bou­tique has lived rent-free in my head for some­thing like twen­ty years, and while I’ll ne­ver in a mil­lion years im­it­ate Death­spell Ome­ga’s com­po­si­tion­al sty­le, they’re pro­bab­ly most re­spon­si­ble for my at­tempts to com­bine cho­ral mu­sic and me­tal.
